Personality and Temperament


The Russian Blue cat is gorgeous to look at, with its soft, silky, short coat of silvery blue-gray, but it also makes an excellent companion. Russian Blues are affectionate and loyal pets who cheerfully accompany their family members around the house, yet they might be hesitant around strangers.

The Russian Blue isn't particularly mischievous. Like any breed, these cats may get into mischief during their kittenhood, but once you've gently taught them which surfaces are theirs to sit on and which should be avoided, the Russian Blue will normally stay to approved locations.

Russian Blue cats are incredibly bright and can learn a variety of skills, including fetch, arriving when called, and more. Many also learn to open doors, which is useful when attempting to keep a cat out of a specific area or cupboard! Russian Blue cats do not often respond well to leash training due to their quiet and reserved nature. They are significantly more content sticking to known quantities and spending time with their families.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

The Russian Blue has no specific nutritional requirements. However, high-quality nutrition is crucial for the health of all cats. Fresh food is an excellent alternative and high-protein, low-carbohydrate commercial foods that feature actual fish or real meat as the major ingredient are preferred to high-carbohydrate diets that can contribute to obesity.

Grooming

The Russian Blue cat's short, easy-care coat means it doesn't require much grooming. These cats benefit from weekly brushing, which removes loose hair and reduces shedding.

Brushing your cat's teeth daily using a pet-safe toothpaste and toothbrush is the most effective way to avoid dental disease, and frequent nail trimming will keep the nails in good shape.

Exercise

Even though Russian Blue cats are known for their dignity, they have a playful side. These cats have a great time with both self-play toys and interactive toys like feathery wands and lasers. They also like a multilevel cat tower, especially if it has sleeping and hiding areas. Scratching posts are also needed.

Health

The Russian Blue is a naturally occurring breed, hence most Russian Blues are in good health. These cats may be susceptible to some of the same feline ailments as other cats, such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, kidney disease, obesity, and periodontal disease.


History


With its stunning silvery-gray appearance, you'd think the Russian Blue cat was painstakingly bred by feline geneticists, but this is a naturally occurring breed. These cats, believed to have originated in Russia's Archangel Isles near the port of Arkhangelsk, most likely accompanied seafarers to Northern Europe and Great Britain in the 1860s. The first Archangel Cat was displayed at London's Crystal Palace in 1875, and since then, these incredible cats have been greatly sought after.

The Russian Blue was shown alongside all other blue cats until it was assigned its own show class in 1912. Following World War II, the Russian Blue saw a population drop, as did many other cat breeds. Breeders, mostly in Scandinavia and England, worked to replenish populations.

Once these cats arrived in the United States, breeders allowed the British Russian Blue and Scandinavian Russian Blue cat bloodlines to mix. At the same time, Russian Blue cats have influenced the development of other breeds, including the Nebelung, also known as the long-haired Russian Blue. The Havana Brown was also produced with the assistance of Russian Blues, and some Oriental Shorthairs have Russian Blue DNA as well.




Did You Know?

Russian Blue cats are sometimes called "Archangel Cats" after their supposed origin in Russia's Archangel Isles.





Russian Blue Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The second layer is rich and luscious, with a dense yet fine texture. Hairs stick out from the body, providing a delicate, velvety feel.


COLOR


Russian Blue cats have an even "bright blue" color, with lighter shades preferred over darker ones. The ground color and tipping are noticeably different, with all guard hairs silver in hue. There are no tabby "ghost stripes" evident. The nose leather is slate grey, while the paw pads are a mauve to lavender pink tint.



BODY


Russian Blue cats are typically medium-sized, with delicate bone structure and a beautiful, athletic appearance.


HEAD


The head has a medium wedge shape, neither too short nor too long. The muzzle is blunt in contour, continuing the head's general wedge shape. There is no evident nose break, nor are there noticeable whisker pads.


EYES


The eyes are round and spaced widely apart. Every Russian Blue cat has bright green eyes.


EARS


The Russian Blue cat's ears are rather large, with wide bases, sharp points, and practically translucent skin. The ears have scant ornamentation, and leather may be seen through the outer hair.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs are long and fine-boned, while the paws are short and somewhat rounded.


TAIL


The Russian Blue cat has a long tail that is proportional to its body. The tail tapers from base to tip.



Did You Know?

During World War II, the Russian Blue breed nearly went extinct, but dedicated breeders in Scandinavia and England helped preserve the bloodline.
