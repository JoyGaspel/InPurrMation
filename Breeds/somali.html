Personality and Temperament


Somali cats are energetic, playful, and very intelligent. They are known for their agility and curiosity, often getting into everything around the house. They are affectionate and form strong bonds with their families but also enjoy a bit of independence. Somali cats are quite social and love interactive play.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

Somali cats require a high-quality, protein-rich diet to fuel their active lifestyles. Look for commercial food with real meat as the first ingredient to ensure they stay healthy and energetic.

Grooming

Their semi-long coat requires regular grooming. Brushing 2-3 times a week will help to prevent matting and tangling, as well as reduce shedding. Keep their nails trimmed and their teeth cleaned as part of their regular care.

Exercise

Somali cats are very active and playful. They require plenty of exercise to stay fit and healthy. Interactive toys, climbing structures, and puzzle feeders will help to keep them engaged and entertained.

Health

Somali cats are generally healthy but may be prone to certain conditions such as progressive retinal atrophy (PRA) and hip dysplasia. Regular veterinary visits are important to monitor their health.


History


The Somali cat is a variation of the Abyssinian and is thought to have originated from the same gene pool. The breed was recognized in the 1960s, and over time, breeders worked to develop the distinct long-haired version we now know as the Somali.




Did You Know?

Somali cats are known for their bushy tails and ticked coats, which give them a wild, fox-like appearance. They are highly intelligent and can be trained to perform various tricks.





Somali Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The Somali has a semi-long, soft coat with a ticked pattern. The coat is easy to maintain with regular grooming.


COLOR


The Somali cat comes in various colors such as ruddy, red, blue, and fawn.



BODY


Somalis possess a medium-sized, athletic, and graceful build. The average weight for the male Somali is 8 to 12 pounds; that for the female is slightly less.


HEAD


The head of the Somali cat is wedge-shaped with a well-defined muzzle. The overall expression is alert and intelligent.


EYES


Somalis have large, almond-shaped eyes that are wide-set and expressive, adding to their exotic look. Their eyes are often amber or green.


EARS


Somali cats have large, pointed ears that are a distinctive feature of the breed.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Somali cat are long, slender, and muscular. The paws are oval-shaped.


TAIL


Somali cats have a long, bushy tail with a slight taper, giving them a fox-like appearance.



Did You Know?

All cats are intelligent, but Somali cats are off the charts. Highly trainable, eager to please, and athletic, Somalis love to learn tricks, particularly when yummy rewards are offered.
