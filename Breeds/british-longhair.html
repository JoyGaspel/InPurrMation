Personality and Temperament


The British Longhair is known for being a calm, easygoing, and affectionate breed. They form close bonds with their owners and enjoy spending time with them, although they are not typically "lap cats." This breed is well-suited to families with children and other pets due to their tolerant and patient nature.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

British Longhairs do not have specific dietary requirements, but it is important to provide them with a balanced diet to maintain their overall health. Due to their tendency to gain weight, it is advisable to feed them a high-protein, low-carbohydrate diet and monitor their caloric intake.

Grooming

The British Longhair's dense, plush coat requires regular grooming to keep it in good condition. Weekly brushing is usually sufficient to remove loose hairs and minimize shedding. During the shedding season, more frequent brushing may be necessary.

It is also important to maintain regular dental care by brushing their teeth and providing dental treats or toys. Regular nail trimming and ear cleaning should be part of their grooming routine.

Exercise

British Longhairs are not overly active cats, but they do enjoy playtime and interactive toys. Providing them with scratching posts, climbing trees, and puzzle toys can help keep them mentally and physically stimulated. Encouraging play sessions can also help prevent obesity.

Health

The British Longhair is generally a healthy breed, but they can be prone to certain genetic conditions such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) and polycystic kidney disease (PKD). Regular veterinary check-ups and genetic testing can help identify and manage these conditions.


History


The British Longhair is a relatively recent addition to the cat fancy, developed by breeding British Shorthairs with Persians. This blend of breeds resulted in a cat with the same robust build and calm temperament as the British Shorthair, but with a long, luxurious coat. The British Longhair is sometimes referred to as the Britannica or Lowlander.





Did You Know?

The British Longhair is known for its plush, luxurious coat and calm, affectionate nature. They are sometimes called "teddy bear cats" due to their cuddly appearance.

British Longhairs are closely related to British Shorthairs and share many of the same personality traits.





British Longhair Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The British Longhair has a dense, plush coat that is long and soft. The texture of the coat is silky and requires regular grooming to prevent matting.


COLOR


British Longhairs come in a wide variety of colors and patterns, including solid, tabby, bicolor, and more. The most iconic color is blue (gray), but they can also be found in black, white, cream, red, and other shades.



BODY


The British Longhair has a sturdy, muscular body with a broad chest and strong legs. They have a well-balanced, cobby build that gives them a powerful and imposing appearance.


HEAD


The head of a British Longhair is round and broad, with a short, straight nose and a strong, well-developed chin. They have full cheeks and a rounded muzzle that gives them a distinctive "smiling" expression.


EYES


The eyes of a British Longhair are large, round, and expressive. They can come in various colors, including copper, gold, blue, and green, depending on the coat color.


EARS


The ears of a British Longhair are medium-sized, with a broad base and rounded tips. They are set wide apart on the head, contributing to the breed's overall round appearance.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of a British Longhair are short and strong, with substantial boning. The paws are round and firm, providing a solid foundation for their muscular body.


TAIL


The tail of a British Longhair is thick at the base and tapers to a rounded tip. It is of medium length and proportionate to the body.
