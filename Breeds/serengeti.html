Personality and Temperament


Serengeti cats are active, intelligent, and playful. They are known for their dog-like personalities and are very social. They enjoy interacting with their human families and can form strong attachments. They are curious and love to explore.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

A high-protein diet with real meat or fish as the primary ingredient is best. Serengetis are active and energetic, so a nutrient-rich diet helps to keep them in top shape.

Their short, sleek coat requires little grooming. Regular brushing will help to keep it shiny and reduce shedding.

Serengetis are highly active and need plenty of playtime to stay happy and healthy. Interactive toys, climbing posts, and scratching pads will help to keep them entertained.

Serengeti cats are generally healthy, but like all breeds, they benefit from regular vet visits to monitor their health and wellness.


History


The Serengeti breed was developed in the early 2000s by crossing Bengals with Oriental Shorthairs. The goal was to create a cat with a wild look but the temperament of a domestic cat. Their striking appearance and energetic nature have made them a popular choice for cat lovers.




Did You Know?

Serengeti cats are known for their striking appearance, which includes large, almond-shaped eyes and a sleek, muscular build. They are a relatively new breed, having been developed in the early 2000s.





Serengeti Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The Serengeti has a short, sleek coat with a soft feel. The coat is easy to maintain with regular grooming.


COLOR


The Serengeti cat can have a spotted or marbled coat with golden, yellow, or silver bases.



BODY


Serengetis possess a medium to large-sized, sleek, and muscular build. The average weight for the male Serengeti is 10 to 15 pounds; that for the female is slightly less.


HEAD


The head of the Serengeti cat is long and wedge-shaped with a prominent nose. The overall expression is alert and intelligent.


EYES


Serengetis have large, almond-shaped eyes that are wide-set and expressive, adding to their exotic look.


EARS


Serengeti cats have large, pointed ears that are a distinctive feature of the breed.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Serengeti cat are long, slim, and muscular. The paws are small and oval-shaped.


TAIL


Serengeti cats have a medium-length tail that is thickest at the base and tapers to a point at the end.



Did You Know?

All cats are intelligent, but Serengeti cats are off the charts. Highly trainable, eager to please, and athletic, Serengetis love to learn tricks, particularly when yummy rewards are offered.
