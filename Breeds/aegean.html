Personality and Temperament


The Aegean cat is an old breed of domestic cat that has always existed naturally, without human breeding. They are not very common outside their home islands. Aegean cats have medium-length fur and a medium-sized body. They are friendly and clever. Over time, they have become fond of people. In Greece and nearby islands, they are valued for catching mice and are often given food by fishermen and locals. They make great family pets because they get along well with almost everyone. Aegean cats are also very talkative and enjoy chatting with people. If you talk to them, they will likely respond with happy sounds like chirps and meows.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Aegean cats don't need special food like some other cats. We recommend giving your cat good quality food. Also, make sure to always have fresh water available. It's normal if your Aegean cat likes to put their paws in the water bowl!

The Aegean cat has a medium-length coat, so you need to groom it regularly. This helps reduce shedding and prevents hairballs. Brush your cat 2 to 3 times a week with a slicker brush to remove extra hair. This will help with shedding and give you time to bond with your cat. Like all cats, the Aegean also needs dental care. You can also treat your pet by clipping its nails regularly.

Aegean cats like to nap more than play and explore. To help your cat get exercise, give them a cat tree that they can also nap on. Toys, especially ones you can move like wands and lasers, can make your cat want to play and help them stay healthy.

The Aegean cat grew on its own without help from others, so it is naturally healthy and has no known health problems.


History


Aegean cats have a long history, dating back thousands of years. They are believed to have come from the Cycladic Islands. Records show that these cats might have been domesticated around 10,000 years ago on Cyprus, near Greece. If this is true, they are one of the oldest cat breeds still around today. DNA studies suggest that Aegean cats are related to Angora and other Turkish breeds. They likely arrived in Greece and the Aegean Islands by hiding on fishing and trading boats. The Aegean cat was officially recognized as a unique breed only in the 1990s, making it both old and new!




Did You Know?

Many of the feral cats roaming Greece and the surrounding islands are Aegean cats. Aegean cats have an instinctive love of the outdoors and can become quite unhappy when kept as indoor-only pets.





Aegean Cat: Breed Information






COAT


All Aegean cats are semi-long-haired, with soft undercoats that are shed during hot weather.


COLOR


Aegean cats come in a variety of bicolor and tricolor patterns, all with white being the dominant color. Black, blue, and red patches either with or without tabby striping are displayed.



BODY


Aegean cats are medium-sized cats with strong, solid bodies that have a lot of muscle.


HEAD


An Aegean cat has a round head with smooth lines. Many Aegean cats have stripes on their face and a clear letter M shape on their forehead.


EYES


Aegean cats have green eyes that can be light or dark. Their eyes are always almond-shaped.


EARS


The average Aegean cat has medium-sized ears with rounded tips.


LEGS & PAWS


The Aegean cat has strong, well-proportioned legs and rounded paws. Paw pads come in different colors.


TAIL


An Aegean cat has a tail that is proportionate to its body, usually wider at the base and slightly tapered toward the tip.



Did You Know?

All cats are intelligent, but Aegean cats are off the charts. Highly trainable, eager to please, and athletic, Aegean cats love to learn tricks, particularly when yummy rewards are offered.
