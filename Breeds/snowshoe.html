Personality and Temperament


Snowshoe cats are known for their friendly and social personalities. They are affectionate and love to interact with their human family members, often following them around and participating in household activities. Snowshoes are intelligent, curious, and enjoy learning new things.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

A high-quality, protein-rich diet is best for Snowshoes. Choose a food where real meat or fish is the first ingredient to support their active and healthy lifestyle.

Grooming

Snowshoes have short, easy-to-care-for coats. Weekly brushing is typically enough to keep them looking their best and reduce shedding. Regular nail trimming and dental care are also recommended.

Exercise

Snowshoe cats are playful and energetic. They enjoy interactive toys and games, and they thrive in environments where they can climb and jump. Provide them with scratching posts and climbing structures to keep them physically stimulated.

Health

Snowshoes are generally healthy, but they may be susceptible to heart issues such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM). Regular vet check-ups are important to catch any potential health issues early.


History


The Snowshoe cat breed originated in the United States in the 1960s. They were created by crossing American Shorthair cats with Siamese cats that had white paws, resulting in their distinctive "snowshoe" appearance.

Snowshoe Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The Snowshoe has a short, dense, and soft coat that is easy to maintain with regular grooming.


COLOR


The Snowshoe cat is mostly white with colored markings on the face, tail, and back.



BODY


Snowshoes possess a medium-sized, muscular, and sturdy build. The average weight for the male Snowshoe is 9 to 12 pounds; that for the female is slightly less.


HEAD


The head of the Snowshoe cat is round with a broad muzzle and a slightly rounded profile. The overall expression is friendly and curious.


EYES


Snowshoes have large, round eyes that are wide-set and expressive, adding to their endearing look. Their eyes are usually blue or green.


EARS


Snowshoe cats have medium-sized, slightly rounded ears that are a distinctive feature of the breed.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Snowshoe cat are medium-length, strong, and muscular. The paws are white and oval-shaped.


TAIL


Snowshoe cats have a medium-length tail that is thickest at the base and tapers to a point at the end.



Did You Know?

All cats are intelligent, but Snowshoe cats are off the charts. Highly trainable, eager to please, and athletic, Snowshoes love to learn tricks, particularly when yummy rewards are offered.
