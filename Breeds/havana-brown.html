Personality and Temperament


Friendly, intelligent, and playful, Havana Brown cats are wonderful all-around pets for families of all types. They love children and are capable of making long-term friends out of other pets. All these wonderful qualities have led to the Havana Brown being nicknamed "Chocolate Delight." These little babies are just plain gorgeous, with stunning chocolate-colored coats and bright green eyes. There is no other cat that resembles a Havana Brown.

If you are looking for a devoted pet, you may want to look at a Havana! These cats shatter feline stereotypes that include aloof, independent natures; in fact, The Havana Brown is a charmer, living on human attention. These babies are not content without their beloved humans by their sides.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

Havana Brown cats have no special nutritional needs. At the same time, it is vital to provide a high-quality diet to support well-being. If you are not feeding fresh food, ensure that real meat or fish is the number one ingredient in the commercial diet you choose.

Grooming

Thanks to their short coats, Havana Brown cats require no intensive grooming. You can keep your cat looking their best by removing loose hair with a soft brush once or twice per week. You might also want to polish your cat's coat with a chamois or silk scarf after brushing. This will really bring out the shine!

You can help your cat stay healthy by teaching them to allow you to brush their teeth. Last but not least, you might consider keeping their nails trimmed. Your furniture and clothing will thank you.

Exercise

Havana Brown cats are very playful by nature. Not only do they like to show off their athletics by acrobatic leaps and high jumps, they appreciate games of fetch and might even bring you a favorite toy to encourage you to join in on the fun.

Treat your cat to plenty of toys and be sure that they have items to keep them engaged when you can't be there to play. Puzzle games, a tall cat tree, multiple scratching posts, and a window seat with a view of a birdfeeder are some of the things that will help keep your Havana Brown cat entertained so they are less likely to get into trouble while you're away.

Health

Havana Brown cats are generally healthy, but those familiar with the breed have identified some issues that tend to occur at a higher than average rate.

Some Havana Brown kittens are born with hemophilia, which prevents blood from clotting properly. This isn't terribly common however it does underscore the importance of choosing a reputable breeder.

Upper respiratory infections and urinary tract problems can occur in any cat but some Havana's appear to be slightly more susceptible to these issues than others. Your veterinarian will be able to guide you on treatment if your pet develops these or any other illness.


History


Although the solid brown cats are spoken of in the Tamra Maew Cat-Book Poems, an old text dating as far back as 1350 c.e. when Siam was established, the Havana Brown cat we are familiar with is quite a modern breed. Those very first old brown cats were not only handsome companions, they were said to have the ability to ward off evil. Since they were thought to be of such immense worth, a few self brown cats were imported to England from Siam in the late 19th century. They were said to be "Siamese, with coats of burnished chestnut, and greeny-blue eyes."

Not all of these early imports were genetically identical to today's Havana Brown cats. Instead, they were probably Burmese, Tonkinese, and chocolate point Siamese. Some may have been the result of pairings between Siamese and domestic black or blue cats, resulting in cats that were similar to contemporary Havana Browns.

Records show that solid brown cats were shown in Europe during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. In 1888, a brown cat was awarded first prize at an English show, providing more evidence that the cat fanciers of the era appreciated these stunning chocolate colored cats as much as we do today.

Havana Brown: Breed Information






COAT


The coat should be smooth and short and feel very soft to the touch.


COLOR


The only colors permitted are chocolate-based. Eumelanistic lilac coloring is permitted by TICA as long as the cat is of a chocolate basis. Nose leather and paw pad color should be deep chocolate to black, matching the color of the cat's coat.



BODY


The body of the Havana Brown is a muscular one which is neither too cobby nor too long. Havana Brown cats are usually heavier than they appear.


HEAD


The head of the Havana Brown should be longer than wide with a good jaw and muzzle appearing almost square in shape. There is a clean break behind the whisker pads.


EYES


The eyes need to be large and oval in shape. The color green in all its variations is permitted; brighter colors are more desirable. Eye color is permissible to be changed in Havana Brown cats within one year.


EARS


A Havana Brown should have wide set ears with rounded tips. The ears are alert and very wide set with minimal hair on the outside and essentially no furnishings.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs are to be long and thin but not fine boned. The paws should be short, with an oval shape.


TAIL


The Havana Brown cat sports a thin, medium-length tail with a tapered tip.
