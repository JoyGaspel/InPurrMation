Personality and Temperament


The Australian Mist has the distinction of being the first pedigreed cat ever developed in Australia.

This beautiful shorthair was first developed by breeding the Abyssinian with the Burmese. The Abyssinian added the playful personality and ticked coat of the breed. The Burmese added its calm temperament and smoothly shaded coloring. Some foundation cats also contained domestic shorthairs, who added a broad amount of genetics which resulted in the development of a healthy cat breed that shows an exquisite diversity of colors.

Australian Mist cats possess beautiful short coats in marbled or spotted tabby patterns. Ticked coats make these cats look soft and shaded, which has given rise to the use of the word "mist" in the breed name.

As a loving, easy-going cat, it is little wonder that the Australian Mist is highly sought after in its home country, and the breed is increasing in popularity on show rings throughout the world.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

The Australian Mist cat does not have any special dietary requirements. We suggest feeding a high-quality, high-protein food that is appropriate for your cat's life stage and activity level.

Grooming

Groom the Australian Mist's short coat once a week with a fine comb or soft brush. Trim the nails every one to two weeks, and brush teeth daily with a pet-safe toothpaste and toothbrush.

Exercise

Australian Mist cats are moderately active. Playful and inquisitive, Australian Mist cats enjoy interactive play with the human family as well as with other pets in the household. Challenge their body and mind with lots of indoor enrichment in the form of scratching posts, cat towers, perches, and toys.

Health

There are no known inherited health issues in Australian Mist cats.


History


The Australian Mist cat was developed in Australia during the 1970s by Dr. Truda Straede from Burmese, Abyssinians, and domestic short-haired cats. The distinctive coat colors and spotted patterns of the cat gave it its initial name: the Spotted Mist. As the breed continued to develop, marbled coat patterns appeared, and in 1998, the official name was changed to Australian Mist.

Australian Mist cats are prevalent all over their native land, yet to date, have not yet been extensively acknowledged across Europe, the United Kingdom, and America. Not recognized by the Cat Fanciers' Association (CFA) at the current time, the Australian Mist is completely accepted by The International Cat Association (TICA).

Its friendly disposition, overall good health, and striking appearance are spreading popularity outside Australia.




Did You Know?

It can reach two years before the Australian Mist matures its coat color.

The Australian Mist is a rare breed of cat. Although it was created in the 1970s, it is still referred to as a "new" breed of cat when compared to others.





Australian Mist Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The Australian Mist has a short, dense coat that is soft and silky to the touch. The coat comes in a variety of colors and patterns, including marbled and spotted tabby patterns.


COLOR


The coat color of the Australian Mist can range from warm brown to cool silver, with various shades in between. The patterns are well-defined, and the coat has a misty appearance, giving the breed its name.



BODY


The Australian Mist cat possesses a medium-sized, muscular, and well-balanced body with a broad chest and rounded hindquarters.


HEAD


The head of the Australian Mist is gently rounded and has a nose break with a broad, short muzzle and a firm chin. The whisker pads are distinct, adding to the breed's unique facial features.


EYES


Large, expressive eyes are a hallmark of the Australian Mist. The eyes are set wide apart and can be any shade of green, complementing the cat's coat color.


EARS


The ears of the Australian Mist are medium to large, slightly rounded at the tips, and set well apart on the head. They have a slight forward tilt, giving the cat an alert appearance.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Australian Mist are sturdy and well-muscled, with the hind legs slightly longer than the front legs. The paws are oval and proportionate to the body size.


TAIL


The tail of the Australian Mist is of medium length, thick at the base, and tapers to a rounded tip. It is well-covered with fur and blends seamlessly with the cat's overall appearance.
