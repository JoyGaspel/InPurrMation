Personality and Temperament


European Shorthair cats are affectionate, playful, and intelligent. They are known for being friendly and love interacting with their human companions and other pets. They are playful and enjoy engaging activities, making them a great choice for families with children.

Their short coat requires minimal grooming. Brushing once a week will help prevent mats and reduce shedding. Regular nail trimming and dental care are also important.

These cats are active and enjoy interactive play. Providing them with toys, scratching posts, and climbing opportunities is a great way to keep them physically and mentally stimulated.

European Shorthair cats are generally healthy but can be prone to certain genetic conditions. Regular vet check-ups and a healthy diet will help keep them in top condition.


History


The European Shorthair, also known as the Celtic Shorthair, is a pedigreed cat breed that originated in Europe. The breed's history is closely intertwined with its role as a skilled working cat, highly valued for its hunting abilities in ancient Rome. Pedigreed European Shorthair cats were developed in Sweden to resemble the typical domestic cats of Europe.

European Shorthair Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The European Shorthair has a short, smooth, and soft coat that is easy to maintain. The coat has a variety of colors and patterns, including those of the Asian Shorthair and Burmese.


COLOR


The European Shorthair cat coat comes in a variety of colors and patterns, including brown, black, tan, and gray. The coat color is uniform throughout the body, with no markings or patterns.



BODY


European Shorthair cats possess a medium-sized, sleek, and muscular build. The average weight for the male European Shorthair is 8 to 15 pounds; that for the female is slightly less.


HEAD


The head of the European Shorthair cat is a rounded shape with a broad, well-defined muzzle. The overall expression is alert and intelligent.


EYES


European Shorthair cats have striking almond-shaped eyes that come in a variety of colors. The eyes are large, expressive, and add to the breed's exotic look.


EARS


European Shorthair cats have large, wide-set, triangular ears that are cupped at the base.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the European Shorthair cat are long, slim, and muscular. The paws are relatively small and oval-shaped.


TAIL


European Shorthair cats have a medium-length tail that is thickest at the base and tapers to a point at the end.
