Personality and Temperament


The American Bobtail cat is known for its friendly, doglike nature and wild appearance. Despite their feral lineage, they are domesticated and boast large vocabularies filled with various sounds. American Bobtails form strong bonds with their human families and get along well with other pets. They are energetic, enjoy jumping and climbing, and can be trained to walk on leashes or play fetch. However, they are also skilled escape artists, so it‚Äôs important to ensure their carriers are securely latched when traveling.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

American Bobtail cats need a meat-based diet because they are obligate carnivores. Feed them measured meals rather than leaving food out all day to prevent overeating and obesity.

Grooming

American Bobtail cats have short and medium-length coats that are easy to care for. Regular brushing keeps their fur looking nice and gives them some attention. Since these cats enjoy climbing, trim their nails regularly to keep them comfortable and safe.

Exercise

American Bobtail cats are active and playful. They enjoy games and love climbing. If you want a cat that interacts well with people and might enjoy walking on a leash, the American Bobtail is a good choice. Provide a climbing tower, a window perch, scratching posts, and a basket of toys. Shiny toys are often favorites.

Health

American Bobtail cats are known for being very healthy. They do not have any specific health problems.


History


The American Bobtail cat is a new breed that developed in the 1960s. Breeders created it using a feral brown tabby male with a short tail and a seal point domestic female. This breed formed through natural selection, primarily with feral domestic cats that had natural bobtails. Different breeders across the United States worked together to develop the American Bobtail, leading to a strong breed with no known genetic defects. The gene for the American Bobtail's short tail is dominant, unlike the Japanese Bobtail, which has a recessive gene for its short tail. The International Cat Association (TICA) recognized the American Bobtail in 1989. It also participates in championships with the Cat Fanciers' Association (CFA) and is recognized by the Cat Aficionado Association of China. The World Cat Federation acknowledges the breed but does not allow it to compete.




Did You Know?

Many of the feral cats roaming Greece and the surrounding islands are Aegean cats. Aegean cats have an instinctive love of the outdoors and can become quite unhappy when kept as indoor-only pets.





American Bobtail Cat: Breed Information






BODY


The American Bobtail cat boasts a robust and elongated physique, exuding a sense of strength and stability. Its chest is impressively broad and full, while its hips are sturdy and substantial, giving the body a powerful appearance. This breed is characterized by its striking rectangular silhouette, which adds to its unique charm and presence.


HEAD


The American Bobtail cat has a broad wedge-shaped head that matches the size of its body. Its head has no flat areas, and there is a smooth curve from the nose to the brow. The muzzle is wide, and the whisker pads stand out. The cat has strong, full jaws.


EYES


The American Bobtail cat has striking, nearly almond-shaped eyes that fit its broad head perfectly. Set deep in the sockets, the eyes are spaced moderately apart and angled toward the base of the cat's ear. Their color beautifully matches the coat, enhancing the breed's unique charm.


EARS


An American Bobtail cat has medium-sized ears with wide bases and rounded tips.


LEGS & PAWS


Like a bobcat, the American Bobtail cat has longer hind legs and slightly shorter front legs. Its large feet have a noticeably rounded shape, and many American Bobtails sport tufts of fur between their toes.


TAIL


The American Bobtail cat is known for its short tail, which typically measures between one-third and half the length of a regular cat's tail. This tail generally does not extend below the hock. While the tail is usually straight, it may also feature acceptable curves, bumps, or slight knots.


COAT


American Bobtail cats may have dense shorthair coats or medium long-haired coats.


COLOR


American Bobtail cats come in a variety of colors and patterns, all of which are acceptable. However, coats that exude a wild appearance‚Äîrich, earthy tones and striking patterns‚Äîare particularly favored, enhancing their captivating presence.



Did You Know?

American Bobtails have a fanciful quirk: They absolutely love shiny objects, and will go to great lengths to steal them. It's not uncommon for owners to find stashes of shiny objects that have been hidden by their American Bobtail cats!

Even though they are big and wild looking, American Bobtail cats are excellent family pets. They love children and they enjoy snuggling. Some people refer to them as the Golden Retrievers of the cat world.
