Personality and Temperament


Playful and clever, and with a touch of a demanding attitude, the Japanese Bobtail is a rare and gorgeous cat with silky coat, beautiful appearance, and a sweet singsong voice.

If you're fortunate enough to have a Japanese Bobtail cat in your life, you'll have a best friend forever. Japanese Bobtails are incredibly loyal and affectionate, yet they think that you exist to serve them. They adore to stay at their people's heels, meowing and pawing at them for attention, and bringing toys to beg to play fetch with them, which can last indefinitely.

Japanese Bobtails are famous for their friendly nature. They are so sociable that they will greet strangers like familiar friends. They adore participating in everything, reading the same books you do, playing video games and surfing the web together, and assisting you with all your projects while engaging in a continuous conversation.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Japanese Bobtail cats have no specific nutritional requirements. We advocate a high-protein diet with less carbohydrates. If you don't feed your cat fresh food, choose a high-quality commercial brand with actual meat or fish as the main ingredient.

Brushing is beneficial to the Japanese Bobtail cat, especially if the cat's coat is medium to long. This reduces shedding and the probability of hairballs. It's a good idea to encourage your cat to accept nail clipping and toothbrushing from an early age.

Japanese Bobtail cats are extremely athletic and will gladly spend hours playing. These cats enjoy running and jumping, and they respond well to walking on leashes. They generally enjoy travels and thrive at feline agility events.

Because they are so busy, Japanese Bobtails require appropriate play equipment such as cat towers and shelves. Cat trees provide a place to climb, while scratching posts protect your furniture from damage.

Japanese Bobtail cats are normally in excellent health, with no known breed-specific genetic diseases.


History


If you assumed that Japanese Bobtail cats originate from the island of Japan, you're right! The original cats to migrate to Japan most likely migrated from China or Korea, circa 600 to 700 AD, when Buddhist monks needed assistance in keeping rats out of the rice paper rolls they used as temple records.

As the bobtail gene spread, there were more bobtail cats. In the 1600s, Japan's silk trade was being overrun by rats and the cats were let out of the temples with the invaluable task of rodent control. All Japanese Bobtail cats trace their origins back to these extremely useful feline workers.

The initial Japanese Bobtails to ship to the United States came over in 1968, assisted by Elizabeth Freret. The Cat Fanciers' Association (CFA) included shorthair Japanese Bobtail cats for championship in 1976. It took much later, in 1993, for the longhair Japanese Bobtail cat to be officially recognized. The International Cat Association (TICA) recognized shorthair Japanese Bobtail cats as competition material in 1979 and the longhair in 1991.




Did You Know?

The Japanese Bobtail's distinctive short tail is the result of a natural genetic mutation, not human breeding intervention.

In Japanese folklore, the "Maneki-neko" or "beckoning cat" figurine is based on the Japanese Bobtail and is believed to bring good luck and fortune.





Japanese Bobtail Cat: Breed Information






BODY


The Japanese Bobtail is small to medium-sized, with straight lines and a clean, muscular appearance. The torso is long and lean, and the overall appearance is one of balance. Males are typically larger than females.


HEAD


The head is nearly perfect in equilateral triangle shape, with gently curved lines, high cheekbones, a long nose, and a distinct whisker break. Males will have jowls.


EYES


The eyes of the Japanese Bobtail cat are large, wide, and alert, with an oval shape. All eye colors are acceptable, and cats with odd eyes are considered desirable.


EARS


The ears are big and expressive. They are positioned upright, wide apart, and at right angles to the head.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs are long and slender, with an athletic appearance. The hind legs are noticeably longer than the forelegs, with deep angles at the joints that make the cat's back appear nearly level when standing. The feet are oval-shaped.


TAIL


The tail of the Japanese Bobtail cat is unique, with one or more curves, kinks, or angles. It can be rigid or flexible, and it blends in with the rest of the cat. The tail has a fluffy appearance, similar to that of a pom-pom.


COAT


The coat can be short or long, but it is always soft and silky and does not require an undercoat. Longhair Japanese Bobtail cats have a frontal ruff, breeches, ear tufts, and tufts of hair between their toes.


COLOR


Every color and pattern is acceptable. The nose leather and paw pad colors complement the coat color.
