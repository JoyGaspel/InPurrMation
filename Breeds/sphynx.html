Personality and Temperament


The Sphynx is a unique and playful breed known for being affectionate, friendly, and outgoing. These cats are often described as "dog-like" in their behavior, as they enjoy being the center of attention and interacting with people. Sphynx cats are highly social and require plenty of interaction with their families.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Sphynx cats have higher energy needs than other breeds due to their lack of a coat. A high-protein diet is important to keep them healthy. Choose a food with real meat or fish as the main ingredient and ensure they have a balanced diet.

Despite having no fur, Sphynx cats require regular grooming. Their skin tends to accumulate oils, so they should be bathed every 1-2 weeks to keep them clean. They also need regular nail trimming and dental care.

Sphynx cats are highly energetic and love to play. They need a variety of toys and activities to keep them mentally and physically stimulated. They enjoy climbing and jumping, so providing vertical spaces will keep them happy.

Sphynx cats are generally healthy but are prone to certain conditions, including hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) and skin conditions. Regular vet visits are essential for monitoring their health.


History


The Sphynx originated in Canada in the 1960s when a hairless kitten was born to a domestic cat. The breed was developed through selective breeding, and the Sphynx has since become known for its wrinkled skin and affectionate nature.




Did You Know?

Sphynx cats are known for their lack of fur, which makes them feel warm and soft to the touch. They are highly social and require plenty of attention and interaction with their families.





Sphynx Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The Sphynx is hairless, with soft, warm skin that has a slight peach fuzz texture. The skin is easy to maintain with regular grooming.


COLOR


The Sphynx cat can come in any color or pattern, as long as the coat is hairless.



BODY


Sphynx cats possess a medium-sized, muscular, and solid build. The average weight for the male Sphynx is 8 to 12 pounds; that for the female is slightly less.


HEAD


The head of the Sphynx cat is round with high cheekbones and prominent cheek pads. The overall expression is friendly and curious.


EYES


Sphynx cats have large, almond-shaped eyes that are wide-set and expressive, adding to their unique look. Their eyes are often green or yellow.


EARS


Sphynx cats have large, wide ears that are a distinctive feature of the breed.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Sphynx cat are long, slender, and muscular. The paws are small and webbed, adding to their agility.


TAIL


Sphynx cats have a long, thin tail that tapers to a point, giving them a sleek appearance.



Did You Know?

All cats are intelligent, but Sphynx cats are off the charts. Highly trainable, eager to please, and athletic, Sphynx cats love to learn tricks, particularly when yummy rewards are offered.
