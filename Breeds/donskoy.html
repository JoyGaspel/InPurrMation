Personality and Temperament


Donskoy cats, also known as Don Sphynx or Russian Hairless, are affectionate, playful, and curious. They are known for being friendly and love interacting with their human companions and other pets. They are playful and enjoy engaging activities, making them a great choice for families with children.

Donskoy cats are generally healthy but can be prone to certain genetic conditions. Regular vet check-ups and a healthy diet will help keep them in top condition.


History


The Donskoy cat breed originated in Russia, specifically from Rostov-on-Don, hence the name. The breed appeared in 1987 when a kitten with a genetic mutation causing hairlessness was discovered by Elena Kovaleva. Although sometimes called the Don Sphynx, the Donskoy is not related to the better-known Sphynx cat (Canadian Hairless), whose hairlessness is caused by a recessive mutation in the keratin 71 gene.

Donskoy (Don Sphynx) Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The Donskoy has a hairless coat that is easy to maintain. The coat has a variety of colors and patterns, including those of the Sphynx and other breeds.


COLOR


The Donskoy cat coat comes in a variety of colors and patterns, making it one of the most visually striking cat breeds. Common colors include black, white, blue, and chocolate, among others.



BODY


Donskoy cats possess a medium-sized, sleek, and muscular build. The average weight for the male Donskoy is 6 to 12 pounds; that for the female is slightly less.


HEAD


The head of the Donskoy cat is a rounded shape with a broad, well-defined muzzle. The overall expression is alert and intelligent.


EYES


Donskoy cats have striking almond-shaped eyes that come in a variety of colors. The eyes are large, expressive, and add to the breed's exotic look.


EARS


Donskoy cats have large, wide-set, triangular ears that are cupped at the base.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Donskoy cat are long, slim, and muscular. The paws are relatively small and oval-shaped.


TAIL


Donskoy cats have a long, slender tail that tapers to a point at the end.
