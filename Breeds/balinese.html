Personality and Temperament


If you're looking for a cat with a soft voice and a playful spirit, the Balinese is an excellent choice. These elegant cats are known for their gentle, melodic voices, making them great conversational companions. They enjoy interacting with their favorite people and will happily ‚Äúdiscuss‚Äù the day's events. Balinese cats are affectionate, friendly, and very intelligent. When properly socialized, they get along well with everyone, including children and other pets, making them a great fit for families. Their sharp minds and curious nature keep their families entertained, and they thrive on mental stimulation. Balinese cats enjoy learning tricks, playing fetch, and many even love taking walks on a leash.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

Balinese cats do not require a special diet, but they benefit from high-quality food that lists real meat or fish as the first ingredient. Natural diets are good for all cats, and this breed is no exception! To keep a Balinese cat's coat soft, supple, and silky, ensure they get enough Omega-3 essential fatty acids, either from their food or through additional supplements.

Grooming

Balinese cats have a long-haired coat, but it is a single coat rather than a double coat. The lack of a thick undercoat reduces the risk of matting. You can help your cat look and feel great by brushing and combing them a few times a week. Regular brushing not only helps bond with your pet but also reduces hairball formation. Since Balinese cats are powerful climbers with an active play drive, consider keeping their nails trimmed to protect your skin and furniture. Lastly, brush your cat‚Äôs teeth regularly or offer treats to help prevent tartar buildup.

Exercise

Balinese cats need plenty of exercise, and most are eager to seek out playtime on their own. Provide a variety of toys to encourage play, and ensure they have perches and climbing spaces. A multi-level cat tree will keep your Balinese happy while protecting your furniture. Balinese cats love their families and thrive on interactive play. Like their Siamese ancestors, they quickly learn to play fetch and often enjoy walking on leashes, allowing them to explore the outdoors safely.

Health

Balinese cats tend to be very healthy and can live to age 15 or longer. However, like their Siamese relatives, they can develop certain health issues, such as chronic bronchial disease and bladder stones. Some may be born with crossed eyes or kinked tails. Additionally, some Balinese cats may have a genetic disorder called lysosomal storage disease, which leads to symptoms like fainting, seizures, balance problems, and a failure to thrive. This disease is fatal and is usually detected within the first few months of a kitten‚Äôs life. Balinese cats can also be prone to feline acromelanism, which causes their coat color to darken with frequent exposure to cold temperatures. This condition isn‚Äôt serious and does not cause other health problems; it simply changes the cat‚Äôs appearance.

The Balinese cat‚Äôs history dates back to the mid-1800s when Siamese cats were brought to the U.S. and the UK from Thailand. Did You Know?

Balinese cats have traditional Siamese coat colors, while Javanese or Balinese-Javanese cats have non-traditional Siamese coat colors including lynx (tabby point) and tortie point colors and patterns. All Balinese kittens are born cream or white, and color develops as they get older.

On average, Balinese cats are a little less vocal as Siamese, and their voices tend to be softer.





Balinese Cat: Breed Information






COAT


Their silky, medium-length coat lies close to the body and lacks a fluffy undercoat, giving it a smooth, flowing texture. The tail is especially eye-catching, with long, feathery plumes that add to their graceful look.


COLOR


Balinese cats come in four classic colors: seal point, chocolate point, lilac point, and blue point. The Javanese color division includes a wider range, introducing shades like cream point.



BODY


The Balinese is a sleek, elegant cat with a long, lean build. It has fine bones and a graceful, athletic frame. Its body is tubular in shape, with hips and shoulders of equal width, giving it a balanced, streamlined look.


HEAD


This breed has a distinctively long, wedge-shaped head that tapers smoothly from the nose to the tips of the ears, creating a striking triangular profile. The nose is long and straight, and there should be no break at the whiskers.


EYES


One of the Balinese cat‚Äôs most captivating features is its deep sapphire-blue eyes. They should sit close together, with just enough space for another eye between them.


EARS


Balinese cats have large, wide-set ears that are broad at the base and taper to a fine point, adding to their regal appearance.


LEGS & PAWS


With their long, slender legs, Balinese cats have a naturally graceful stance. Their hind legs are slightly taller than the front, giving them a poised, elegant look. Their small, oval paws add to their dainty charm.


TAIL


Their long, thin tail tapers to a fine point, with soft, flowing fur that fans out like a delicate plume, adding to their overall elegance.
