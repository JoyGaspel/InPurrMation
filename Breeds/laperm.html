Personality and Temperament


The LaPerm cat's adorable personality goes beyond its lovely, curly coat. If you meet this adorable kitty even once, you will fall in love!

LaPerm cats make excellent family pets, adoring everyone from toddlers to grandparents. They also enjoy socializing with other pets, such as felines and well-behaved dogs. These kittens enjoy cuddling up, whether on your lap, next to you, or under your covers.

Between naps, these gorgeous felines keep themselves active. They enjoy both helping their people with projects and enjoying with their toys. The LaPerm cat has an inquisitive temperament and will readily investigate unsecured cupboards and drawers, investigating and perhaps removing the contents.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

The LaPerm cat has no unique nutritional requirements; nevertheless, you may want to choose a food with additional Omega fatty acids to preserve good skin and maintain the colorful, curly coat that distinguishes this breed. In addition, make sure you select a high-quality food that is strong in protein and low in carbohydrates. Real meat or fish should be the primary element.

Grooming

While the LaPerm cat is low-maintenance, it is recommended that they be groomed at least twice or three times each week with a comb or brush with revolving bristles. The texture of your cat's coat will decide how frequently your assistance is required.

In addition to coat care, consider clipping your cat's claws on a regular basis. While LaPerm cats are not very destructive, keeping your cat's toenails short will help protect your furniture.

You may also wish to train your cat to tolerate toothbrushing, as regular dental care promotes overall health.

Exercise

LaPerm cats are naturally energetic. In addition to sprinting and jumping, they enjoy climbing more than the ordinary person. If you adopt one of these amazing kittens, make sure to provide them with many cat trees and consider adding cat-friendly shelving on the walls.

LaPerm cats want to watch their homes from the highest possible vantage point, and without these items, they are more inclined to locate less acceptable perches, such as the tops of cupboards and refrigerators.

Like many other cat breeds, the LaPerm tends to slow down with age. Because inactivity can lead to weight gain, keep them active with puzzle toys, feathery wands, catnip mice, and other favorites.

Health

LaPerm cats are known for their superb health. This breed is not prone to any known hereditary disorders.


History


The LaPerm is a relatively new cat breed. In 1982, a farm cat in Oregon gave birth to a kitten with almost no fur. As the kitten grew bigger, she developed a curly coat, so her adoptive, Linda Koehl, a cat lover, named her "Curly."

When Curly grew up, she had a few litters of kittens, some of whom got curly coats like their mother. Curly's owner became interested in feline genetics and eventually determined that her cat's distinctive appearance was caused by a dominant gene.

Curly and her kids were quite popular at shows, and curiosity in this unusual breed rose. The International Cat Association (TICA) gave official recognition in 2002. The Cat Fanciers Association (CFA) followed suit, as did many other cat breed registries throughout the world.




Did You Know?

LaPerm kittens are often born bald or nearly bald, developing their distinctive curly coats as they grow.

The LaPerm's curly coat is caused by a dominant gene, meaning only one parent needs to carry the gene for the trait to appear in offspring.





LaPerm Cat: Breed Information






BODY


LaPerm cats are medium-sized, weighing between 5 and 8 pounds. Males tend to be slightly larger than females. The overall image is of a fine-boned but not delicate cat with plenty of muscle and graceful toys.


HEAD


The head is a modified wedge shape with subtle rounding and soft curves. The whisker pads should be large and spherical, with long whiskers. The whiskers are usually curling.


EYES


The eyes are medium-sized, attentive, expressive, and slightly slanted. They should be almond-shaped when the cat is comfortable, and rounder when the cat is attentive. Eye color has nothing to do with coat color.


EARS


LaPerm cats have medium-to-large ears with full furnishings. The long-haired version should wear earmuffs and lynx tips.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs are long and thin, giving the impression that the cat is walking upright. The hindlegs may be longer than the front legs. The paws should be round.


TAIL


A LaPerm cat's tail is unique and should match its coat. Shorthaired variations should have tails that resemble bottle brushes, and longhaired individuals should produce a plume.


COAT


The coat is the LaPerm cat's defining trait. It could be long or short, and it might alter over time. In shorthaired variants, the coat has a silky, wavy appearance and feel. Long-haired LaPerm cats frequently grow ringlets and a longer mane around the chest area.


COLOR


LaPerm cats come in every color and pattern conceivable.
