Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Bombays do well on a high-protein, meat-based diet. A balanced diet with proper nutrients, including omega fatty acids, will help support their health and coat. Fresh food with minimal fillers is always a good choice for this breed.

Bombays have a short, sleek coat that requires minimal grooming. Brushing once a week will help maintain their shiny coat and minimize shedding. Regular nail trimming, ear cleaning, and dental care are also part of the grooming routine.

Bombays are active cats that love to play and explore. They enjoy interactive play and will happily chase after toys or play games like fetch. Providing them with climbing opportunities, such as cat trees and shelves, will help keep them entertained.

Bombays are generally healthy cats but can be prone to some genetic conditions, such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) and respiratory issues due to their short noses. Regular vet checkups will ensure they stay healthy.


History


The Bombay was developed in the U.S. in the 1950s by breeding a black American Shorthair with a Burmese cat. COAT


The Bombay cat has a short, glossy, black coat with a sleek, shiny appearance.


COLOR


The Bombay cat has a solid black coat with a deep sheen.



BODY


The Bombay cat is medium-sized and muscular with a compact build.


HEAD


The head of the Bombay cat is round and broad with a short, wide muzzle.


EYES


The Bombay cat has large, round, gold to copper-colored eyes.


EARS


The ears of the Bombay cat are medium-sized and rounded, set wide apart.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Bombay cat are medium-length with rounded paws.


TAIL


The Bombay cat has a medium-length tail with a slightly tapering tip.
