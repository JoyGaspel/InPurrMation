Personality and Temperament


Singapura cats are affectionate, playful, and curious. They are known for their intelligence and agility. Despite their small size, they are energetic and enjoy engaging in interactive play with their owners.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

A balanced, high-protein diet is ideal for this breed, ensuring they stay healthy and maintain their energy levels. High-quality commercial food with real meat as the first ingredient is a good choice.

Grooming

Singapura cats have a short coat that requires minimal grooming. Brushing once a week should be sufficient to remove loose fur.

Exercise

Singapura cats are very active and love to explore. They will need plenty of toys and climbing spaces to keep them entertained.

Health

Singapura cats are generally healthy but can be prone to dental issues, so regular dental care is important.


History


The Singapura is one of the smallest cat breeds, and it originates from Singapore. They were brought to the United States in the 1970s and were recognized as a breed in 1988.




Did You Know?

Singapura cats are known for their small size and big personalities. They are one of the smallest cat breeds and are often referred to as "miniature lions" due to their playful and curious nature.





Singapura Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The Singapura has a short, fine coat that is silky to the touch. The coat is easy to maintain with minimal grooming.


COLOR


The Singapura cat has a warm, sepia agouti coat with darker points on the ears, tail, and face.



BODY


Singapuras possess a small and compact, muscular build. The average weight for the male Singapura is 6 to 8 pounds; that for the female is slightly less.


HEAD


The head of the Singapura cat is rounded with a short muzzle. The overall expression is gentle and curious.


EYES


Singapuras have large, almond-shaped eyes that are wide-set and expressive, adding to their endearing look. Their eyes are typically green or yellow.


EARS


Singapura cats have large, pointed ears that are a distinctive feature of the breed.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Singapura cat are short, slim, and muscular. The paws are small and oval-shaped.


TAIL


Singapura cats have a medium-length tail that is thickest at the base and tapers to a point at the end.



Did You Know?

All cats are intelligent, but Singapura cats are off the charts. Highly trainable, eager to please, and athletic, Singapuras love to learn tricks, particularly when yummy rewards are offered.
