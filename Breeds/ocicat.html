Personality and Temperament


Despite its wild appearance, the Ocicat is completely domestic, making it suitable for individuals seeking a cat with exotic appearances and a tame, friendly demeanor. The Ocicat is named by its striking similarity to the ocelot. It has a short coat that is easy to care for, and its temperament is so sociable that it is frequently referred to as a dog in a cat's body.

The Ocicat's joyful, outgoing nature is derived from the breeds that formed its basis, Siamese and Abyssinian. This remarkable cat can walk on a leash, play fetch, and learn tricks and commands often associated with dogs rather than cats.

This fun-loving cat prioritizes having a good time and will gladly accompany its owners wherever they go. Nowhere is off limits: Many Ocicats adore water and enjoy accompanying their human friends to the shower or bath, dipping curious paws into the water to ensure that everything is in order. Ocicat owners with koi ponds and aquariums must frequently go to tremendous measures to keep their finned pets safe from fishing.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

Ocicats have no unique nutritional requirements, although they do require high-protein, high-quality meals that include real meat and fish.

Grooming

The Ocicat's sleek, short hair is easy to care for. A weekly brushing should be sufficient to remove any loose hairs and avoid excessive shedding.

Brushing your cat's teeth and cutting their claws on a regular basis will help minimize damage to furniture.

Exercise

Ocicats are incredibly athletic, and they have no problem zooming around the home, jumping to the highest view points, and climbing anything they can.

Health

Ocicats are normally healthy, but they can develop several known health concerns like as progressive retinal atrophy, renal amyloidosis, and hypertrophic cardiomyopathy. These are not typical issues, but they are worth keeping an eye out for.


History


The Ocicat originated as a hybrid when Virginia Daly, a Michigan cat breeder, crossed an Abyssinian with a Siamese in an attempt to generate an Abyssinian-pointed Siamese. Tonga, a spotted kitten, was born via matings between second-generation cats with Abyssinian points.

Tonga was neutered and enjoyed life as a pet, but breeders attempting to produce more Abyssinian-pointed Siamese cats took notice of the beautiful spotted offspring that frequently occurred, and interest steadily increased until separate breeding programs were formed using the Siamese to Abyssinian formula, followed by breeding offspring of the first pairing to Siamese. The first "official" Ocicat, Dalai Dotson, was registered with The Cat Fanciers Association (CFA) in 1966.

Subsequent generations were the product of breedings between Abyssinians, Siamese, and American Shorthair: CFA made a mistake while documenting breed information, first stating that Ocicats were produced with Abyssinian and American Shorthair parents. When they attempted to remedy the blunder by adding "Siamese," they did not remove American Shorthair. The incorporation of American Shorthairs in the foundation resulted in greater sizes and the addition of silver to the previously existing hues.




Did You Know?

The Ocicat was created by accident when a breeder was trying to produce an Abyssinian-pointed Siamese cat.

Despite their wild appearance, Ocicats are 100% domestic cats with no wild blood in their lineage.

HEAD


The head is a modified wedge form with a small curve extending from the muzzle to the cheek. The chin should be solid, and the muzzle should be well-defined, slightly square in shape. Mature guys may exhibit jowling.


EYES


The Ocicat's eyes should be large and almond shaped. They should be slightly angled upward, toward the ears. Eyes may be any hue other than blue.


EARS


The ears should be fairly large and positioned at a 45-degree angle. Ear tufts occasionally emerge but are not essential.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs should be medium-length, muscular, and proportionate to the body. Feet should be compact and oval-shaped.


TAIL


An Ocicat's tail should be long and thin, tapering slightly at the end and with a black tip.


COAT


The coat should be flat and close to the body. The texture should feel satiny and silky.
