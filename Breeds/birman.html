History


The Birman, also known as the "Sacred Cat of Burma," has an intriguing history. According to legend, these cats were temple guardians in Burma, loved by the priests and revered for their golden eyes and striking color points. The modern Birman was developed in France in the 1920s, with the breed becoming popular in the U.S. in the 1960s.

Birman Cat: Breed Information






COAT


The Birman cat has a semi-long, silky, and dense coat with a slight ruff around the neck.


COLOR


The Birman cat has a cream or fawn-colored body with darker color points on the ears, face, paws, and tail.



BODY


The Birman cat is medium to large, muscular with a strong and solid frame.


HEAD


The head of the Birman cat is broad and rounded with a strong chin.


EYES


The Birman cat has large, almond-shaped, deep blue eyes.


EARS


The ears of the Birman cat are medium-sized and slightly rounded at the tips.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs of the Birman cat are medium-sized with rounded paws.


TAIL


The Birman cat has a medium-length tail with a slight taper toward the end.
