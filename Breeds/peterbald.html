Personality and Temperament


What happens when you cross an Oriental shorthair and a Donskoy cat? A Peterbald cat! These colorful creatures are easy to care for, with fun-loving, vibrant dispositions and incredibly appealing features.

The Peterbald cat possesses a sense of loyalty that many other felines lack; in fact, this cat is frequently compared to a dog because it is so friendly with its humans.

These cats are quite friendly, and they will enthusiastically meet visitors rather than lurking cautiously when guests visit. Because of their sociable nature, they are unhappy when left alone, hence they want regular companionship. The good news is that companionship does not have to be human: Peterbald cats, like their Oriental cousins, may create strong ties with other felines and dogs.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

Peterbald cats have no unique nutritional requirements; nevertheless, they do require a high-quality diet that includes actual fish or meat as the primary element. Depending on their activity level, these kittens may require slightly more food than the normal cat simply because they burn more calories with their antics.

Grooming

The Peterbald, like all other hairless cats, requires an occasional bath. This is important since there isn't enough hair to absorb all of the skin's oils. Excess oil attracts dirt, causing irritation and odor. A mild shampoo and a warm, soft place to dry are two must-haves for Peterbald cat owners.

Exercise

You will not need to persuade the Peterbald cat to play! These frolicsome kittens retain their youthful zest well into adulthood and are always eager to have fun with a friend or by themselves. A tall cat tree, scratching posts, and a toy basket are must-haves.

Peterbald families will want to ensure that their cats always have something appropriate to entertain themselves with; without cat-friendly needs, these kitties will manufacture their own fun, frequently with objects that their human pals would like to keep on a high shelf or in a drawer.

Health

Peterbald cats are generally healthy, although some are prone to a skin disorder known as feline ectodermal dysplasia. Even without this unpleasant skin disease, these cats are vulnerable to damage if mistreated by children, competing cats, or overeager canines.

They must be treated tenderly and protected from the weather because they are susceptible to sunburn, windburn, and other skin disorders caused by exposure.


History


In 1994, a cat breeder in St. Petersburg, Russia, intended to create a completely new breed that combined the best of two of her favorites. Olga Mironova successfully bred a hairless Donskoy with an Oriental Shorthair, resulting in a cat that looked like a Sphynx but was genetically closer to an Oriental Shorthair with a hair-losing gene.

In 1996, the Russian Selectional Feline Federation (SFF) accepted the Peterbald cat. The International Cat Association (TICA) approved the breed in 1997, and the World Cat Federation (WCF) accepted it in 2003. The American Cat Fanciers Association (ACFA) approved the Peterbald in 2008. The breed is gaining popularity globally because to its exceptional personality, yet it is quite uncommon in contrast to other cat breeds.



Did You Know?

The Peterbald was created by crossing a Donskoy (Don Sphynx) with an Oriental Shorthair, combining the hairless gene with the elegant body type of Oriental breeds.

Peterbald Cat: Breed Information






COAT


Peterbald cats may have long or short coats.


COLOR


Peterbald cats can come in a wide variety of colors and patterns, ranging from solid hues to striking tabbies and unique calicos, in both long-haired and short-haired varieties. This diversity allows for a beautiful range of appearances within the breed. Additionally, the color of their nose leather and paw pads complements their coat colors, enhancing their overall aesthetic and adding to their charm.



BODY


The Peterbald cat features a lean and elegant body, characterized by moderate muscle mass and a slender build. Female Peterbalds typically weigh between 5 and 8 pounds, while males generally weigh between 7 and 10 pounds. This balanced weight contributes to their graceful movement and overall appearance.


HEAD


The Peterbald cat has a modified wedge-shaped head that is longer than it is wide, with no flat planes. Its nose is of moderate length, featuring a straight, slight rise from the bottom of the eyes to the forehead, followed by a gentle curve toward the top of the head.


EYES


The Peterbald cat has walnut-shaped eyes that are oval on top and rounder at the bottom. These eyes are set at a slight angle between the tip of the nose and the base of the ears, and they are positioned one eye width apart.


EARS


The Peterbald is named for its uniquely curled ears, which have an arc of 90 to 180 degrees. These ears are wider at the base, open in shape, and have rounded, flexible tips. They are moderately large and evenly placed on the sides and top of the head, with desirable ear furnishings adding charm.


LEGS & PAWS


The legs should be of medium length, with medium boning. The feet should have a rounded shape and should be of medium size.


TAIL


The Peterbald's tail should be the same length as its body. It should be wide at the base with a tapering shape.



Did You Know?

Peterbald cats come in five coat types: bald, flock, velour, brush, and straight-coated, with only the first four being acceptable for showing.
