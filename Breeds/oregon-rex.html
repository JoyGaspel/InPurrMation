Personality and Temperament


The Oregon Rex cat was known for its affectionate, playful, and intelligent nature. They were highly social and enjoyed interacting with their human companions. Oregon Rex cats were known for their loyalty and formed strong bonds with their families. They were relatively quiet and not overly vocal.



Nutrition Grooming Exercise Health

Nutrition

The Oregon Rex thrived on a balanced diet that included high-quality protein sources. Fresh food with minimal fillers was always a good choice for this breed. Ensuring they had access to clean water at all times was essential for their health.

Grooming

The Oregon Rex had a short, curly coat that required minimal grooming. Brushing occasionally helped maintain their coat and minimized shedding. Regular nail trimming, ear cleaning, and dental care were also part of the grooming routine.

Exercise

Oregon Rex cats were active and enjoyed interactive play. Providing them with toys, scratching posts, and climbing opportunities helped keep them physically and mentally stimulated.

Health

Oregon Rex cats were generally healthy but, like all breeds, could be prone to some genetic conditions. Regular vet checkups ensured they stayed healthy.


History


The Oregon Rex cat breed originated in the United States, with the first documented case of a kitten with short, curly fur born in Oregon in 1955. The breed experienced a surge in popularity during the 1960s but eventually waned. Breeders lost interest, and the last known purebred Oregon Rex cat is believed to have died in 1972, leading to the breed's extinction.





Did You Know?

The Oregon Rex cat breed, known for its unique curly fur, was developed by outcrossing curly-coated cats with domestic cats to prevent inbreeding. Despite its initial popularity, the breed eventually went extinct in the early 1970s.
